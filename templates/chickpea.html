Significance of Chickpea:

Chickpea is a major pulse crop, constituting about 49% of the total pulses produced in India. It is grown across various agro-climatic zones in almost every part of the country. Chickpeas are rich in protein, fiber, complex carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals, making them a nutritious food choice. They are known for their efficient utilization of soil moisture and nutrients, as well as their positive impact on soil properties.

Varieties and Research:

More than 250 chickpea varieties have been developed in India to date. These varieties exhibit higher yield potential, reduced crop duration, large-seeded kabuli types, and resistance to biotic and abiotic stresses. Research efforts have led to improved chickpea varieties suitable for different regions.

Profit Potential:

On average, a farmer can obtain around 8 quintals of chickpea crop per acre. Selling at the market rate (e.g., Rs. 3,325 per quintal), the total income from 1 acre of chickpea cultivation can be approximately Rs. 26,6001.
