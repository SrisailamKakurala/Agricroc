Kidney bean cultivation is also popular in India. Major states for growing kidney beans include Himachal Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, Kerala, Karnataka, Maharashtra, West Bengal, Uttarakhand, Uttar Pradesh, and Tamil Nadu. Hereâ€™s how to start growing kidney beans for high profits:

Select a Good Location:

Choose a location with full sun exposure and excellent drainage. Kidney beans are sensitive to saline soils, so ensure good soil quality. Optimal soil pH for kidney beans is between 5.5 and 6.

Prepare the Soil:

Kidney beans grow well in loamy soil that is well-drained and slightly rich in organic content. Plough the field 1-2 times and add both organic and chemical fertilizers. For commercial production, apply 80-100 kg Nitrogen and 50-60 kg Phosphorus per acre. Level the soil after preparation.

Seed Requirement:

On average, you will need about 30-35 kg seeds per acre for early-sown varieties and 10-12 kg per acre for pole-type varieties. Planting and Care: Sow kidney bean seeds at a depth of 2-3 inches. Maintain proper spacing between plants (around 20-25 cm). Control weeds during the growing season. Kidney beans require regular irrigation.

Harvesting:

Harvest kidney beans when the pods are fully mature and dry. Allow the pods to dry on the plant before harvesting. Thresh the dried pods to separate the beans.

Profit Margin:

The profit margin for kidney beans can be calculated using the formula: Profit Margin (%) = (MSP - Cost of Cultivation) / Cost of Cultivation * 100 Refer to the National Conference on Agriculture and Agricultural Statistics for specific data on cost of cultivation and MSP for kidney beans13.
