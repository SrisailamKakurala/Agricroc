Area Under Cultivation:

India has witnessed a steady increase in grape cultivation over the years. As of 2020-21, the total area under grape cultivation was approximately 1.05 lakh hectares. Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh are the leading grape-producing states in India.

growth and demand:

The demand for grapes has surged due to their health benefits, versatility, and use in winemaking. Grapes are consumed fresh, dried (raisins), and processed into juices, wines, and jams. Varieties:

India cultivates various grape varieties, including Thompson Seedless, Sonaka, Sharad Seedless, and Anab-e-Shahi. Thompson Seedless is the most popular table grape variety due to its sweet taste and seedlessness.

Climatic Requirements:

Grapes thrive in warm and dry climates. Sunlight, well-drained soil, and proper irrigation are essential for successful grape cultivation.

Training and Pruning:

Proper training and pruning techniques enhance grape yield and quality. Techniques like cane pruning and trellis systems are commonly used.

Pest and Disease Management:

Common pests include mealybugs, thrips, and mites. Diseases like powdery mildew and downy mildew can affect grapevines. Integrated pest management (IPM) practices are crucial.

Harvesting:

Grapes are harvested when they reach the desired sugar content (Brix level). Hand harvesting is common for table grapes.
